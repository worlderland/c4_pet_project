Travel experiencing & enjoying locals with AirBnB

G'day fellow travellers!
Today I would like to talk about something that has literally revolutionized the way we travel and that of course millennials, backpackers and travellers of any kind are loving: AirBnb

AirBnB is a creative company who has basically created a worldwide accommodation community: they allowed normal people like me and you, with extra rooms at their places, or extra properties, to actually rent them out as holiday accommodations, and vice-versa, travellers like us to experience their holiday in a different way, by living with or being in touch with a local.
Once the holiday is over, both host and guest will be asked to review and star each other; poor hosting and rude guests will gain very low ratings, making it very difficult to host and book again; generally nobody books a 1 starred accommodation and on the other side, hosts have the power to refuse your booking just by looking at your stars rating and reviews.

What you probably didn't know is that being a social network based service, AirBnb has an affiliate referral program that works through the sharing of a sign-up link; let me explain in simpler words:

Friends travellers referral program

Every AirBnB traveller customer has a page like mine (above), that is accessible by either clicking on the tab "Credits" or on the profile picture (top-right of your screen) and then "Invite a friend";
What you'll find is your unique advertising link, that you shall use to invite friends into the AirBnB program.
Here there is mine: www.airbnb.com.au/c/alessandroc2289.
Through their cookies AirBnB will know that you are the person who referred a new customer and therefore they will reward you with AirBnB credits once your referred friend/person has booked and enjoyed his/her holiday.
Remember that there is a limit or credits you can acculmulate, which is specified in AirBnB's terms & conditions.

On the other end, as mentioned above, you could also refer a Host:

Friends hosts referral program

As an AirBnB customer you can also refer friends/people to becoma a host; you will be able to access to your host referral link page by clicking on the profile picture (top-right of your screen) and then "Refer Hosts".
This program works pretty much in the same way as the traveller program and will the AirBnB credit rewards.

In conclusion, I can say again that AirBnB is probably my best way to book accommodation while traveling around the world, as I really enjoy meeting and talking to locals; real people with real stories, which they will certainly share with you, making you a little bit richer day by day.

I hope this article helped you clearing up your mind...

Enjoy AirBnB!
